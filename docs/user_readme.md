Web3 Incident Shield: Comprehensive User Guide
1. Introduction
Welcome to Web3 Incident Shield, your all-in-one platform for managing security incidents in the Web3 ecosystem. This application is designed to help your organization proactively identify risks, define clear response procedures, manage active incidents, and conduct thorough post-incident analysis. By centralizing your incident management processes, Web3 Incident Shield empowers your team to respond effectively and minimize the impact of security events.

2. Getting Started
Web3 Incident Shield is a web-based application. Simply open it in your browser to begin. All your data is stored locally within your browser's storage, ensuring privacy and quick access.

3. Navigating the Application
The application's main navigation is located in the sidebar on the left. Each section is dedicated to a specific aspect of incident management:

Dashboard: Your central overview.
Risk Assessment: Identify and manage potential threats.
Security & Audit: Track compliance, audits, and vulnerabilities.
Communication Plan: Define communication protocols and templates.
Response Playbook: Create detailed incident response procedures.
Active Incidents: Manage ongoing security events.
Incident History: Review past incidents and post-mortems.
AI Analysis: Leverage AI for deeper incident insights.
Preparedness Report: Generate comprehensive reports on your readiness.
4. Core Features and How to Use Them
4.1 Dashboard
The Dashboard provides a quick overview of your incident preparedness status. It displays key statistics and offers quick links to important sections of the application.

System Status: Indicates the overall health of your incident management system.
Active Incidents: Shows the number of ongoing incidents.
Quick Actions: Provides direct links to frequently used features like Risk Assessment, Security Checklist, Communication Plan, Response Playbook, Active Incidents, and AI Analysis.
4.2 Risk Assessment
This section allows you to identify, evaluate, and manage potential security risks to your Web3 infrastructure.

Adding a New Risk:

Navigate to Risk Assessment from the sidebar.
Click the Add Risk button.
In the dialog, fill in the following details:
Risk Name: A concise name for the risk (e.g., "Smart Contract Vulnerability").
Description: A detailed explanation of the risk.
Impact Level: Select "Low," "Medium," or "High" to indicate the potential severity if the risk materializes.
Likelihood: Select "Low," "Medium," or "High" to indicate the probability of the risk occurring.
Mitigation Strategy: Describe the steps or controls in place to reduce the risk.
Click Add Risk to save.
Editing/Deleting a Risk:

Locate the risk you wish to modify in the list.
Click the Edit (pencil icon) button to open the edit dialog. Make your changes and click Update Risk.
Click the Delete (trash can icon) button to remove the risk.
4.3 Security & Audit
This section helps you manage your security compliance, track audits, and monitor vulnerabilities. It is divided into three tabs: Security Checklist, Audits, and Vulnerabilities.

4.3.1 Security Checklist

This tab provides a checklist of essential security practices.

Navigate to Security & Audit and select the Security Checklist tab.
Review each item (e.g., "Multi-signature wallet setup," "Smart contract audit").
Check the box next to an item once it has been completed. The progress bar at the top will update to show your overall compliance percentage.
4.3.2 Audits

This tab allows you to record and track security audits conducted by third parties.

Adding a New Audit:

Navigate to Security & Audit and select the Audits tab.
Click the Add Audit button.
In the dialog, provide:
Auditor Name: The name of the auditing firm.
Status: Select "Scheduled," "In Progress," "Completed," or "Delayed."
Start Date: The date the audit began.
Completion Date: (Optional) The date the audit was completed.
Report Title: The title of the audit report.
Report URL: (Optional) A link to the full audit report.
Click Add Audit to save.
Editing/Deleting an Audit:

Locate the audit entry in the list.
Click the Edit (pencil icon) button to modify details.
Click the Delete (trash can icon) button to remove the audit entry.
4.3.3 Vulnerabilities

This tab helps you track identified security vulnerabilities and their remediation status.

Adding a New Vulnerability:

Navigate to Security & Audit and select the Vulnerabilities tab.
Click the Add Vulnerability button.
In the dialog, fill in:
Vulnerability Name: A concise name (e.g., "Reentrancy in withdraw function").
Description: Detailed explanation of the vulnerability.
Source: Where the vulnerability was identified (e.g., "Internal audit," "Bug bounty").
Severity: Select "Critical," "High," "Medium," or "Low."
Status: Select "New," "Acknowledged," "In Progress," "Resolved," or "Deferred."
Remediation Notes: Steps taken or planned to fix the vulnerability.
Click Add Vulnerability to save.
Editing/Deleting a Vulnerability:

Locate the vulnerability in the list.
Click the Edit (pencil icon) button to modify details.
Click the Delete (trash can icon) button to remove the vulnerability.
4.4 Communication Plan
This section helps you define and manage your organization's communication strategy during incidents. It includes tabs for Channels, Team Roles, Templates, and Principles.

4.4.1 Channels

Manage the platforms and links for your incident communication.

Adding a New Channel:

Navigate to Communication Plan and select the Channels tab.
Click the Add Channel button.
Provide the Platform (e.g., "Twitter/X," "Discord") and the Channel Link/URL.
Toggle Primary Channel if it's a main communication platform.
Click Add Channel to save.
Editing/Deleting a Channel:

Locate the channel in the list.
Click the Edit (pencil icon) button to modify details.
Click the Delete (trash can icon) button to remove the channel.
4.4.2 Team Roles

Assign specific communication roles to team members for incident response.

Assigning a New Role:

Navigate to Communication Plan and select the Team Roles tab.
Click the Assign Role button.
Select the Communication Role (e.g., "Lead Communicator," "Technical Lead").
Choose the Assigned Team Member from your pre-defined team members.
Describe the Responsibilities for this role during an incident.
Click Assign Role to save.
Editing/Deleting a Role Assignment:

Locate the role assignment in the list.
Click the Edit (pencil icon) button to modify details.
Click the Delete (trash can icon) button to remove the assignment.
4.4.3 Templates

Create pre-written communication templates for various incident scenarios.

Adding a New Template:

Navigate to Communication Plan and select the Templates tab.
Click the Add Template button.
Enter a Template Name (e.g., "Initial Incident Acknowledgment").
Write the Template Content. You can use placeholders like {incident_type}.
Select Suggested Channels where this template might be used.
Click Add Template to save.
Editing/Deleting a Template:

Locate the template in the list.
Click the Edit (pencil icon) button to modify details.
Click the Delete (trash can icon) button to remove the template.
4.4.4 Principles

Document your organization's overarching communication principles and guidelines.

Navigate to Communication Plan and select the Principles tab.
Type or paste your communication principles into the large text area.
Click Save Principles to store your guidelines.
4.5 Response Playbook
This section allows you to define detailed incident response procedures for different types of security incidents.

Adding a New Playbook:

Navigate to Response Playbook from the sidebar.
Click the Add Playbook button.
The dialog is organized into tabs:
Basic Info:
Incident Type: Select or enter the type of incident (e.g., "Smart Contract Vulnerability").
Description: A detailed description of this incident type.
Trigger Conditions: What conditions or indicators would trigger this playbook.
Response Steps:
Add Initial Assessment Steps, Technical Response Steps, and Post-Mortem Steps. For each step, provide a description and assign a team member.
Communication:
Define the Communication Strategy for this incident type.
Add Communication Snippets (pre-written messages) with descriptions and content.
Legal & Risks:
Note any Legal Considerations relevant to this incident type.
Click Create Playbook to save.
Editing/Deleting a Playbook:

Locate the playbook in the list.
Click the Edit (pencil icon) button to modify details.
Click the Delete (trash can icon) button to remove the playbook.
4.6 Active Incidents
This is where you monitor and manage ongoing security incidents in real-time.

Creating a New Incident:

Navigate to Active Incidents from the sidebar.
Click the Create Incident button.
In the dialog, provide:
Incident Type: Select from your defined incident types.
Severity Level: Select "Critical," "High," "Medium," or "Low."
Assigned Team Members: Select the team members responsible for this incident.
Communication Drafts: (Optional) Initial thoughts or drafts for public communication.
Click Create Incident to save.
Managing an Active Incident:

Each incident card provides an overview and tabs for detailed management:

Status Updates: Use the dropdown next to the incident to change its status to "Active," "Monitoring," or "Resolved."
Add Log Entry: Click the Plus (plus icon) button next to the status dropdown to add a new log entry.
Log Entry: Describe what happened, actions taken, or observations.
Triage Status: Update the triage status (e.g., "New," "Acknowledged," "In Progress," "Resolved").
Assignee: Assign a team member for this log entry.
Ticket/Issue Link: Link to an external ticket system.
Tags: Add relevant tags for categorization.
Overview Tab: Displays incident ID, duration, and number of log entries.
Incident Log Tab: Shows a chronological list of all log entries. You can add new entries directly from here as well.
Team Tab: Lists assigned team members.
Communication Tab: Displays any communication drafts associated with the incident.
Editing/Deleting an Incident:

Locate the incident in the list.
Click the Edit (pencil icon) button to modify its core details.
Click the Delete (trash can icon) button to remove the incident.
4.7 Incident History
This section allows you to review past incidents and conduct post-incident analysis. It is divided into Historical Incidents and Post-Incident Reviews.

4.7.1 Historical Incidents

This tab lists all incidents that have been marked as "Resolved" or "Monitoring."

Navigate to Incident History and select the Historical Incidents tab.
Each incident card provides a summary.
Click View Incident Details to see a detailed breakdown, including assigned team, communication notes, and a link to any associated post-incident review.
If an incident does not yet have a review, you can click Add Review directly from the incident card.
4.7.2 Post-Incident Reviews

This tab is for creating and managing comprehensive reviews of past incidents.

Creating a New Post-Incident Review:

Navigate to Incident History and select the Post-Incident Reviews tab.
Click the Add Review button.
The dialog is organized into tabs:
Overview:
Incident: Select the historical incident this review pertains to.
Review Date: The date the review is conducted.
Executive Summary: A high-level summary of the incident and its resolution.
Analysis:
Root Cause Analysis: Detailed analysis of what caused the incident.
Lessons Learned: Key insights and takeaways.
Governance & Process Retrospective: Review of processes and organizational response.
Action Items:
Add Action Items with descriptions, assignees, due dates, statuses, and optional ticket links.
Click Create Review to save.
Editing/Deleting a Post-Incident Review:

Locate the review in the list.
Click the Edit (pencil icon) button to modify details.
Click the Delete (trash can icon) button to remove the review.
4.8 AI Analysis
This powerful feature allows you to generate comprehensive incident analyses using various AI models.

Configuring AI Provider API Keys:

Navigate to AI Analysis from the sidebar.
Click the API Settings button.
For each AI provider (Google Gemini, OpenAI GPT, Anthropic Claude), enter your respective API key.
You can toggle the eye icon to show/hide your API key.
Click Done to save. Note: API keys are stored locally in your browser for security.
Generating an AI Analysis:

Navigate to AI Analysis and select the Generate Analysis tab.
Select Incident: Choose an incident from your active incidents list to analyze.
AI Provider: Select your preferred AI model (Gemini, OpenAI, or Anthropic).
Custom Analysis Prompt (Optional): You can provide specific instructions to the AI, or leave it blank to use the default comprehensive analysis prompt.
Click Generate Analysis. The application will process the incident log and generate a detailed report.
Viewing Analysis History:

Navigate to AI Analysis and select the Analysis History tab.
All generated analyses are listed here.
Click View Analysis Details to expand and read the full report, including Incident Report, Root Cause Analysis, Lessons Learned, Strategic Implications, Recommendations, and Risk Assessment.
You can Copy the analysis to clipboard, Download it as a JSON file, or Delete it.
4.9 Preparedness Report
Generate comprehensive reports on your Web3 incident preparedness and response capabilities.

Generating a New Report:

Navigate to Preparedness Report from the sidebar and select the Generate Report tab.
Report Configuration:
Report Type: Choose a pre-defined report type (e.g., "Comprehensive," "Executive Summary," "Technical Assessment"). This will pre-select relevant sections.
Report Title: Enter a title for your report.
Description (Optional): Add a brief description.
Output Format: Select the desired output format: "HTML Report," "Markdown," "JSON Data," or "PDF Document."
Report Sections:
Select or deselect individual sections to include in your report. Required sections are marked with an asterisk and cannot be deselected.
Click Generate Report. The report will be generated and automatically downloaded in your chosen format.
Viewing Report History:

Navigate to Preparedness Report and select the Report History tab.
All previously generated reports are listed.
You can Preview a report (HTML format only), Download it again, or Delete it from your history.
5. Data Management
Web3 Incident Shield stores all your data locally in your browser's storage. This means your data is private and accessible only to you.

Export Data: To back up your data or transfer it, use the Export Data as JSON function (usually found in a global settings or data management section, though not explicitly shown in the provided UI, it's available via useData context). This will download a JSON file containing all your application data.
Import Data: You can import a previously exported JSON file to restore your data. Be cautious: importing data will overwrite your current local data.
6. Best Practices for Web3 Businesses
To maximize the value of Web3 Incident Shield for your business:

Proactive Risk Assessment: Regularly update your risk assessment to reflect new threats and changes in your infrastructure.
Comprehensive Playbooks: Develop detailed playbooks for all foreseeable incident types. Ensure they are regularly reviewed and updated.
Team Readiness: Keep your team member information and communication roles current. Conduct regular drills based on your playbooks.
Accurate Logging: Encourage detailed and timely log entries during active incidents. This data is crucial for post-incident analysis and AI insights.
Leverage AI Analysis: Utilize the AI analysis feature to gain deeper insights into incident root causes, lessons learned, and strategic implications. This can inform your continuous improvement efforts.
Regular Reporting: Generate preparedness reports periodically to track your progress, identify areas for improvement, and communicate your security posture to stakeholders and auditors.
Data Backup: Regularly export your data as a JSON file to ensure you have backups in case of browser issues or if you need to transfer your data to another device.
By following this guide and integrating Web3 Incident Shield into your security operations, your Web3 business can significantly enhance its resilience against security incidents.
